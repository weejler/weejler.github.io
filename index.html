Design Project One

GI Joe

This design project involved modifying an ostomy bag to fufill our client's needs. My group decided to create a functioning drainage attatchment to ensure our client would able to sleep through the night.

Overview in Brief

We used Autodesk Inventor to create a locking mechanism that was hotglued to an existing ostomy bag with a vacuum bag to help pull waste through the system.

Learning in Brief

I practiced hard skills (cutting, hot-gluing, 3d printing) as well as my presentation skills.

Reflection in Brief

This was a large change from the structure of highscool right out of the gate, I'm glad I could work in this manner after a long year spent online.

Design Report

Reflection - Lessons Learned

This design project was my first in person experience in over a year and it was initially difficult to readapt. Fortunately it provided a great chance to hone my group work after such an extended isolated period and through weekly milestones I learned how to cooperate as a team again. My 3MT also provided a great opporunity to work on my presentation skills which I took advantage of. I also developed my contrsuction skills practicing with many machines and tools such as the 3d printers and hot glue guns. My iggest shortcoming of the project was the start where I became far to busy far to quickly and ended up missing a submission. This taught me a valuable lesson in time managment and I have not made a simialr mistake since.

Why did you approach the project how you did?

Frequent group meetings and throwing myself into the assignment was a direct response to my initial lack of confidence following the pandemic.

What's a stand out moment?

For me the most exciting experience of the whole project was watching the 3d printer work. I've always considered them so high-tech that I would even understand how to use them, who thought I'd be ready in the first project!

Why did you choose this solution?

It's difficult to pin down exactly when the drainage idea became our main path forwards. I often find the solution to a design problem either jumps out at you like this project or, more commonly, requires many iterations and failures defore you see any form of success.

What is your biggest takeaway?

I used to be someone who vastly preffered to work by myself but this project taught me the value of teammates as I would have been completely unable to produce even a low-fidelity prototype by myself while balancing other course work

What if you had no impact on the outcome?

If I hadn't been an active member of the group or been given any authority to make decisions the project would liekly look very different. Including a belt to help conceal the device and creating a vacuum sealed waste container were both ideas of mine heavily featured in the final design.

What if you could redo it?

While I certianly put all my effort into the project the first time around I believe there is still room for improvement. Many of the good ideas that we had only cropped up towards the end of the project and were left sorley underexplored or poorly made as a result. Given the chance I would be sure to implement these features earlier on.
