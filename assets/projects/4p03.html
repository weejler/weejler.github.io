The Challenge

The **Rehabilitation Center for Children in Winnipeg** faced a dual problem: long waiting times leading to patient discomfort, anxiety, and clinic staff overwhelm. Our capstone project focused on redesigning the waiting room experience not just aesthetically, but through the implementation of a novel, economically viable, and socially beneficial technological solution. The goal was to provide a calming, engaging, and informative waiting environment.

Image: Conceptual rendering of the redesigned waiting room solution.

The Solution and Impact

We developed a modular, interactive system centered around **gamified distraction and information delivery**. This solution utilized low-cost embedded systems and a user-friendly interface to provide calming interactive projections and educational content relevant to the center's services. Key aspects included:

  • **Cost-Effective Design:** Focused on using readily available, maintainable components to ensure long-term sustainability and economic viability.
  • **Patient Engagement:** Interactive elements were designed to reduce perceived waiting time and alleviate stress, especially for children.
  • **Social Benefit:** The system provided direct, measurable improvements in patient well-being and reduced the burden on clinic staff.
