Robotics Motor Driver and Feedback Loop Design

Project Goals and Design

The objective was to design a motor control board capable of driving five high-torque servos with extreme accuracy for a custom-built industrial robotic arm. This required precise current sensing, robust heat dissipation, and a fast, reliable feedback mechanism.

Control System Details

  • **Control Method:** Developed a custom Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) control algorithm in C++.
  • **Driver Circuitry:** Implemented advanced H-bridge driver circuitry optimized for minimal switching loss and thermal efficiency.
  • **Safety Features:** Integrated thermal monitoring and overcurrent protection at the hardware level.
  • **Results:** Bench testing showed a 15% improvement in overshoot and steady-state error compared to off-the-shelf controllers.
